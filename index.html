The Comittees

PM's Cabinet

Agenda: Enforcing an effective quarantine to mitigate the spread of COVID-19 .


In response to the COVID-19 outbreak in India, PM Narendra Modi decided to enforce a mandatory ‘Janta Curfew’ on the nation, and then establish a 21-day curfew given the rapid rise in the number of confirmed cases. This led to mass panic-buying, and most essential services (public transport, banks, etc.) are closed now. Furthermore, this 21-day curfew means that a significant proportion of the population, which works in low-paying jobs, like taxi-driving and managing small shops, has no means to continue earning money.

UN Security Council

Agenda: Investigating the prospect of COVID19 being a bioweapon engineered by the PRC


There have been countless allegations against the People’s Republic of China stating that COVID-19 is a bioweapon engineered to create an economic vacuum that will be filled by the already stabilizing Chinese economy - pushing it into the driving seat. A dramatic fall in confirmed cases in China, along with mild outbreaks in Russia and North Korea, who are Chinese Allies, is held as evidence. If these allegations turn out to be true, China will be violating various international agreements and the consequences will be severe.

ECOFIN

Agenda: Cushioning the economic impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic.


The COVID-19 outbreak has had an unprecedented economic effect on the world at large. It has already hampered the world economy, raised unemployment levels, and tested the healthcare infrastructures of various nation-states drastically. Furthermore, billions of workers around the globe have been forced to work from home, leading to a decrease in productivity levels. Significantly, factories, power stations, and electric grids run the risk of not having their workers and operators come to work.

World Health Organisation

(a) Containing the spread of the COVID-19 Pandemic.

(b) Establishing means to aid countries in slowing down a rapid outbreak .


Having infected more than half a million people and killed 24000 people at the time of the writing of this document, COVID-19 has morphed into a significant threat. With a viable vaccine still a year away at best, there is a lack of effective measures to curb the rapid spread of the virus. One potential solution could be sped up the vaccine design process by removing the need for clinical trials on humans and animals, and testing potential vaccines on infected patients directly. This, however, would raise significant ethical concerns.
